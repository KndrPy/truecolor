# TrueColor Reproducibility Policy

Every canonical stage is executed as a closed scientific unit.

A stage cannot advance merely because code executes. It must establish
entry conditions, data admissibility, estimands, primary analysis,
independent validation, falsification, kill-condition adjudication,
immutable artifacts, hashes, code commit, reproduction command, and
downstream inheritance rules.

Negative findings, falsified hypotheses, and justified non-testability
are complete scientific outcomes. Partial closure is prohibited.

Raw governed medical, biometric, or licensed datasets must not be
committed to the repository. Only reviewed, deidentified, reproducible,
and hash-verified derived evidence may be committed.

All stochastic procedures must use registered seeds. Changes to frozen
seeds, methods, thresholds, datasets, or stage dependencies require a
preregistered deviation record.

Legacy results may inform canonical work but do not become canonical
evidence until they are independently audited, re-executed, or formally
admitted under the canonical artifact contract.
