# TrueColor Stage 1 Closure — Prior-Art Claim Lock

## Decision

Stage 1 is closed against the 18-paper corpus already reviewed in Stage 0. The literature boundary is now operationalized into machine-readable claim, method, dataset, equation-object, novelty, and kill-condition registers.

The project is barred from claiming novelty for generic RGB-to-spectrum reconstruction, metamerism, low-dimensional skin spectra, neural reconstruction architecture, camera calibration, camera sensitivity estimation, ITA-based tone estimation, polarization imaging, layered skin inversion, CRB/wavelength optimization, or the existence of dermatology subgroup disparity.

## Defensible scientific center

TrueColor will test five tightly coupled physical questions:

1. What fraction of measured real-skin spectral variation is observable through realistic camera operators?
2. How frequent and severe are physically plausible skin metamers under realistic noise and calibration uncertainty?
3. How much apparent reconstruction comes from the measurement versus the population prior?
4. How do projection, photon, camera, illuminant, tissue-collinearity, specular, ISP, and data-quality mechanisms contribute and interact?
5. What minimum additional measurement contracts uncertainty under cost and subgroup-parity constraints?

The clinical bridge is separately falsifiable: a physics-derived recoverability score must explain error beyond lesion contrast, label quality, duplicate leakage, prevalence, and acquisition shift, and must replicate in DDI-2.

## Hard scope boundary

Current assets can support information bounds, observability, metamer analysis, spectral geometry, physical-model falsification, sensor design, capture-robustness tests, and clinical association tests. They cannot validate a universal consumer RGB-to-true-spectrum or RGB-to-true-color corrector because no held asset pairs a consumer image and instrument spectrum of the same skin patch.
